Throughout the creation of my game, AI was used as a tool for me to learn and help support during the development process, rather than fully generating my code. AI was useful to me in three different ways. First, I used AI to understand certain concepts that we may not have fully gone over in class, or I simply didn’t have knowledge of. For example, I asked AI how to implement features such as partial name matching for player guesses, creating a “close” hint system for numerical values, and structuring a win streak tracker across multiple games. In addition, AI helped generate ideas of what I could add to my game to make it look better. For example, the guess history section AI helped me come up with the original idea, but then I changed it to how it could fit my game. The last thing I used AI for was to help troubleshoot errors or any bugs that I came across while developing my code.

One of the most helpful aspects of using AI was understanding how to approach problems. Rather than copying lines of code for the solution, I would read the explanation, then adapt and rewrite the code in my own version so that it fits my project. This helped me better understand a lot of the main concepts from the course, such as loops, conditionals, and functions. However, there were some limitations with AI. I figured out that AI cannot always be reliable and can provide incorrect or outdated information. I ran into this problem when developing my CSV file that contained all the player data information. Originally, to save time, I was going to have AI generate me a list of MLB players that I could use for my game. I quickly realized that a lot of the information was wrong, such as the age or the team the player was on. This experience showed me that AI is not always reliable and should be used as a guide rather than just blindly following it.

To ensure the code I submitted is my own, I made sure to understand every piece of code I included. When given AI-generated suggestions, I made sure to modify them to fit my program’s structure and logic, then add my own features and improvements. I also included comments in my code indicating where AI helped guide my approach. Overall, I used AI as a learning tool to further my learning and improve my understanding of the main concepts developed in this course.
